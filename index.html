Meet the Characters

Mei

A determined runner from Taiwan who seeks to understand the world through movement. Her journey of self-discovery takes her across continents as she documents her experiences in her blog "Run the World."

Mei

Passionate about running since childhood, Mei uses movement as her way to process life. Her journey across continents becomes both a physical and emotional odyssey, teaching her that sometimes the most important destination isn't a place, but a person.

Ethan

A travel journalist with a gift for words who keeps crossing paths with Mei. What begins as coincidence evolves into something deeper as he finds himself drawn to her unique perspective on life.

Ethan

The Story

CHAPTER I: THE DEPARTURE

The rhythmic thud of running shoes against pavement was the only sound Mei had trusted for years. In the pre-dawn light of Taipei, she makes a decision that will change everything—trading security for the unknown.

"I run because standing still terrifies me more than any distance."

— Mei

With nothing but a backpack and her running shoes, Mei begins documenting her journey in a blog called "Run the World." Each post becomes a digital footprint, marking her path across continents.

CHAPTER II: COLLISION COURSE

Paris. A crowded café. A spilled coffee. A ruined map. Their first meeting is a disaster—or perhaps, the beginning of something neither of them saw coming.

"You've destroyed my route."

"Maybe some routes are meant to be redrawn."

As Mei continues through Europe, Ethan keeps appearing in her life—a marathon in Italy, a hostel mix-up in Morocco, a midnight run in Tokyo. Coincidence becomes connection, until a betrayal threatens to tear them apart.

CHAPTER III: STANDING STILL

In the misty mountains of rural China, Mei finally stops running. Under a canopy of ancient trees, she realizes that some journeys aren't measured in miles, but in moments.

"You've been searching the world for something that was inside you all along."

— Ethan

The film closes with Mei and Ethan running side by side—not away from something, but toward a shared horizon. Two heartbeats finding their rhythm together.
